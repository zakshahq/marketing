02 The Service

Zaksha is a managed, DNS-based content-filtering service. We let you block categories of content and specific sites, and set time- and group-based schedules, across the devices you enrol — and review a record of activity from a single dashboard. Filtering is applied by directing a device's DNS resolution through Zaksha; you set up each device using the guided steps we provide for iOS, Android, macOS and Windows.

The Service is operated for you as a managed service. Dashboard statistics are computed in periodic batches (roughly every 30 minutes) and are not a live or real-time feed.

03 Best-effort filtering & bypassability

You acknowledge that DNS-based filtering is a best-effort control, not an absolute one, and that its effectiveness depends on the device and how it is managed:

  • It is strongest on cooperative, managed or owned devices — for example a young child's device, or company- or school-owned devices enrolled via MDM where the configuration cannot be removed and VPN installation is restricted.
  • On an adversarial or personal (BYOD) device, filtering can be bypassed: anyone able to change the device's DNS settings, install a VPN, or otherwise tunnel traffic can route around it.
  • You are responsible for protecting device settings (for example with a Screen Time or device passcode and restrictions on VPN installation) where you need stronger enforcement.

The Service is not a substitute for parental, guardian or employer supervision. It is one layer of protection, and you agree not to rely on it as a guarantee that particular content will always be blocked or that a determined user cannot circumvent it.
